Industry Experience

Critical Reflection

In July 2023 I began an industry placement at a software company, in which I joined the development team as a Software Engineer. In this role I was tasked with developing an API and authentication workflow to integrate with company products. During this placement, I encountered a series of experiences and challenges that prompted me to critically examine my actions and their impact on the project. Ultimately this helped foster a sense of self-awareness and a commitment to continuous improvement.

There are two key experiences from this placement that challenged my traditional values and project decision making: API design/development changes and assisting a different team on their project.

Developing the project was largely an independent role, in which I applied problem solving skills to navigate knowledge gaps in programming languages. I drew on learning experiences from previous assessments and employed systematic code review as a research method to enhance my understanding of the project codebase. The project structure was based on a previously developed schema, which slightly differed from a similar company product.

The existing product had more features which would have made my project more challenging for the client to navigate, but would be beneficial for the industry partner to implement for future growth. I evaluated the advantages and drawbacks of incorporating this design and opted to include these additional modules in the codebase, while keeping them optional and hidden from the client's perspective. This approach preserved the logic for future use but ensured a simple and efficient user experience for the client as previously designed.

Throughout the placement, various members of the development team were tasked with different projects. One project was undergoing a major milestone that required further assistance with quality assurance. I was asked to help with this task and had the opportunity to utilise testing skills I had acquired throughout my Computer Science degree. This task was a challenge in terms of ensuring my primary project did not fall behind its time schedule. It meant that I had to adapt original plans to incorporate new tasks. It did however introduce me to a different side of the software development life cycle.

My actions to update the project schema were influenced by both personal characteristics and environmental learning. My traditional approach to thinking tends to be structured and methodical, often involving extensive planning to ensure project adherence. In the placement environment where real clients are involved however, this mindset is not always realistic. It is significantly different to a university project environment where the client is purely theoretical.

My thoughts and actions when assisting on a different project were also shaped by personal traits. Traditionally I’m more familiar with working either independently or as part of a group on a set project, rather than transitioning between multiple deliverables. My limited experience with this type of dynamic working environment coupled with my structured thinking approach, presented challenges as I navigated completely different projects within the same working day. I likely experienced this pressure because I hadn't adequately prepared for unforeseen interruptions. This could have included setting scheduled time at the end of each week for additional/unrelated project assistance. This experience has deepened my understanding of the importance of strong planning and adaptability in achieving the company's overarching goals. It has become evident that certain projects may demand immediate attention to ensure client satisfaction.

I highly value these experiences and their effect on my placement and course learning outcomes. I have come to appreciate the balance between structured thinking and adaptability, and upholding professionalism to ensure client satisfaction. While my inclination towards methodical planning has its benefits in more structured university work, I now recognise the importance of flexibility in real-world software development, especially when dealing with diverse projects and unexpected interruptions. While this adaptability may add personal pressure, I value the need to remain receptive to unforeseen events that can occur within a project. This newfound understanding has deepened my appreciation for considering the long-term implications of design decisions, striking a balance between immediate client satisfaction and future growth of a product.

My new understanding of product design tactics and pre-planning will be important for me in my future career as a Software Engineer and in general life. Practising code review to better understand similar products, and furthermore catering for future development will better equip myself for meeting client requirements and improving business growth. This is likely applicable to all software projects that I will undertake in the future, and I must continue to hone these skills to achieve the most out of my projects whilst maintaining ethical conduct. Life in general is unpredictable, and these experiences will encourage me to practice pre-planning for unexpected events. I can achieve this by brainstorming potential scenarios and contingency plans, whilst embracing a flexible approach to challenges, such as large financial and career decisions.

Ethical Conduct

Throughout my ICT industry placement, I encountered several experiences that prompted critical reflection on the importance of professional accountability and ethical conduct in the industry. One instance revolves around responsible data handling. In this scenario, I was involved in creating, and managing access to, client and industry partner account information. This data was used in an authentication workflow to secure a RESTful API with Azure’s Active Directory.

Poor data handling in this scenario could have resulted in unauthorised access to the API endpoints, potentially allowing external users to capture data and impersonate clients, posing significant security and privacy risks. To mitigate this risk, I ensured all client-identifiable data was not recorded in any API documentation, and data masking was used for all sensitive data entries.

I created training and awareness documentation for the respective clients outlining how to handle their credentials to access the API. This included information on Microsoft recommended libraries for authentication workflows, to discourage direct HTTP request implementation.

A second instance of ethical awareness in my industry placement revolves around upholding professionalism. Throughout the placement I participated in daily standup meetings, where I communicated my work plans and progress with the project team. Furthermore, I was required to create and deliver API workflow diagrams to the project team for communications with the client.

To uphold professional conduct in these situations, I prioritised respectful and inclusive communication strategies. This included active listening and being open to team member feedback on my work. For documentation, I ensured it followed the company formatting policy to maintain consistency and professionalism. These strategies worked well, as they helped create a transparent and efficient working environment. Team members felt heard and respected because of positive communication strategies, and work was delivered to the expected standard.

One aspect that I could have improved upon was seeking feedback earlier in the development process, for better time management. The daily standup meetings are a great opportunity to clarify work, however I appreciate that further discussions may be necessary. It is important to identify when further help is required to maintain project time scheduling.
