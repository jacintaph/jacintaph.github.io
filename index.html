About Me

I’m a Software Engineer, Physiotherapist, and Computer Science student based in Brisbane, Australia. Originally from a South-East QLD town called Bundaberg, I grew up loving sport and the outdoors. I quickly took an interest in a career that could connect me to these passions and became a Musculoskeletal Physiotherapist.

My interest in technology developed from here. Working in a clinic I started to take note of how we used software to manage our day, and more prominently what I wish we could change. Unexpectedly I grew to love researching software development, and after some time decided to make the large career switch. I still play sport regularly, and am very happy to have the skills in sport injury management moving forward.

I undertook a Bachelor of Computer Science majoring in Software Development, and as of this year began a new career path as a Software Engineer in Brisbane whilst I complete my final year of study.

What I look forward to most when working is planning a design and building it from the ground up to see it come to life. My goal is to become proficient in handling both front-end and back-end development as a Full Stack developer.

My recent work industry placement had an enormous positive impact on this goal. I learned crucial software development skills, and I am driven to apply this knowledge in future projects. It has encouraged me to continue learning about my interests and seeking different ways to develop.
